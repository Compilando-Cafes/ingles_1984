About the Book

1984 is George Orwell’s chilling portrayal of a totalitarian society ruled by constant surveillance, public manipulation, and enforced conformity. Set in a grim future, the novel follows Winston Smith as he dares to question the Party’s control.

About the Author

Eric Arthur Blair, known by his pen name George Orwell (1903–1950), was an English novelist and journalist. Famous for his lucid prose and outspoken political views, Orwell created enduring works such as Animal Farm and 1984, warning against the dangers of authoritarianism.
